## Score Level 3 — Below craft floor

**Opening (bestseller-literary register — WRONG voice for this plugin):**

> It was 11:40 on a Tuesday night when Grace finally put her phone face-down on the kitchen counter. The message from her sister glowed behind her eyes anyway — twelve years of resentment compressed into two blue lines. Somewhere between the hum of the refrigerator and the silence of the sleeping house, she wondered whether forgiveness was even possible for people like her.
>
> Perhaps, as some have suggested, forgiveness is less an event than a process.

**Why this scores 3:**
- Clarity of Point: 0 — narrative warm-up opener; no thesis in the first 150 words. DAG-01 auto-revise fires.
- Scripture Saturation: 0 — no scripture block anywhere near the opening.
- Structural Parallelism: 0 — no numbered structure in a designated list chapter.
- Direct Address: 1 — third-person narration about "Grace"; the reader is a spectator, not the addressee.
- Simplicity: 1 — sentence average fine, but "Perhaps, as some have suggested" is a banned hedging hit. DAG-06 auto-revise fires.
- Emphasis & Repetition: 0 — no key statement, no CAPS-in-quote, no anaphora.
- Illustration Discipline: 1 — the scene is literary scene-setting (sensory detail for its own sake), the exact anti-pattern.
- Novelty/Variation: (manuscript-level, assume 0-1)

Total: ~3/16. This is competent bestseller prose — and a complete failure of THIS voice.

## Score Level 9 — Weak but recognisable

**Opening (right shape, weak execution):**

> *And be ye kind one to another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, even as God for Christ's sake hath forgiven you.*
> -- Ephesians 4:32
>
> Forgiveness is important for every Christian. In this chapter we will look at seven reasons why you must learn to forgive. Many people struggle with forgiveness and it affects their lives in different ways.
>
> **1. You must forgive because God has forgiven you.**
>
> This is clear from the scripture above. When you think about how much God has forgiven you, it becomes easier to forgive other people.

**Why this scores 9:**
- Clarity of Point: 2 — anchor scripture + plain declaration; the thesis is unmistakable.
- Scripture Saturation: 1 — block present with reference, but no ALL-CAPS operative phrase, and the restatement is thin ("This is clear from the scripture above" interprets nothing).
- Structural Parallelism: 1 — numbered bold full-sentence point, but "In this chapter we will look at" is banned meta-scaffolding, and the stem is not yet established as parallel.
- Direct Address: 2 — "you" throughout, command implicit.
- Simplicity: 2 — plain, short, no hedging.
- Emphasis & Repetition: 1 — no standalone key statement, no anaphora; "Many people struggle... in different ways" is generic AI filler.
- Illustration Discipline: 0 — no illustration, no concrete anchor anywhere.
- Novelty/Variation: (assume pass, 1-2)

Total: ~9/16. The skeleton is right; the flesh is generic.

## Score Level 14 — Strong (target register)

**Opening (authentic register — composed for calibration):**

> *And be ye kind one to another, tenderhearted, FORGIVING ONE ANOTHER, even as God for Christ's sake hath forgiven you.*
> -- Ephesians 4:32
>
> Unforgiveness is a poison that you drink yourself, hoping that someone else will die. Many Christians are weak, sick and joyless for one reason only: they are holding an offence. It is time to let it go!
>
> **1. Forgive because God has forgiven you of far worse.**
>
> Notice the words of this scripture: "even as God for Christ's sake hath forgiven you." In other words, the standard of your forgiveness is the forgiveness you have already received. Has God forgiven you? Has He blotted out things nobody else knows about? Has He received you back after you failed Him again and again? Then who are you to keep a brother in the prison of your resentment?
>
> Years ago, I knew two brothers who served together in the same fellowship. One of them was overlooked for a position and he never forgot it. He attended, he sang, he even prayed — but something in him had closed. Twenty years later he was still attending and still closed, and the blessing of God seemed to flow around him to everyone else. Unforgiveness does not punish the offender. It punishes you!
>
> Do not forget this: unforgiveness is a poison that you drink yourself.

**Why this scores 14:**
- Clarity of Point: 2 — anchor scripture with CAPS on the operative phrase, thesis declared in the first prose sentence.
- Scripture Saturation: 2 — block quoted, CAPS emphasis, terse introduction, "In other words" restatement, applied to "you".
- Structural Parallelism: 2 — bold full-sentence point heading opening a parallel-stem list ("Forgive because...").
- Direct Address: 2 — you-saturated, a four-question volley, command closers ("It is time to let it go!").
- Simplicity: 2 — short declaratives, zero hedging, no hard words.
- Emphasis & Repetition: 2 — a coined aphorism stated, then restated verbatim as a definition-refrain ("Do not forget this: ..."); exclamation punchlines.
- Illustration Discipline: 2 — one anonymised third-party anecdote, ~90 words, time-marker opener ("Years ago, ..."), lesson stated explicitly at the end as an exclamation.
- Novelty/Variation: (assume 2 — the aphorism would be declared as a chapter-scope refrain)

Total: ~14/16. This is the register every chapter must hit.

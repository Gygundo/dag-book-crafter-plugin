## Score Level 3

A chapter that scores 3/14 on the rubric looks like this (pre-craft, sermon-like — the Phase 7 post-mortem baseline).

**Opening paragraph (paraphrased, statistical opener, no scene, no provenance):**

> Three out of four believers struggle with assurance of salvation at some point in their walk. This is not a small problem. Let us consider what the scripture teaches about grace and identity, because until we understand what is happening underneath the doubt, we cannot hope to address it.

**Why this scores 3:**
- Opening Engagement: 0 — no named human, no time marker, no sensory detail; opens with a statistic and a thesis.
- Emotional Connection: 0 — no vulnerability, no personal anecdote, no first-person beat anywhere in the opener.
- Reader Engagement: 1 — one oblique "we" reference, no direct "you", no rhetorical question.
- Pacing Variety: 1 — two medium-length sentences plus one long one; no short beat to break the rhythm.
- Chapter-Ending Momentum: 1 — (implied: chapter ends on a summarising declaration rather than a hook).
- **Craft Density: 0** — no central image anywhere in the opening zone; no author vulnerability beat with a seeded pointer; both sub-checks fail.
- **Cross-Chapter Craft: 0** — (manuscript-level: Greek terms recur identically across every chapter — charis/pistis/agape in all five openers — lexical fatigue fails; central images are either missing or all gravitate to the same "standing on solid ground" metaphor across chapters).

Total: 3/14. Band: below craft floor — revision required.

## Score Level 6

A chapter that scores 6/14 on the rubric looks like this (mid-upgrade, some craft landed but density and cross-chapter craft still weak).

**Opening paragraph (paraphrased, named human, time marker, partial sensory, still abstract-heavy):**

> Sarah checked her phone at 2am. She had been reading John 10 for an hour, hoping one verse would finally feel true. The words on the screen were familiar. The doubt was familiar too. Grace, the pastor had said on Sunday, is a gift. But what if the gift hadn't been given to her?

**Why this scores 6:**
- Opening Engagement: 2 — named human (Sarah), time marker (2am), sensory detail (phone screen).
- Emotional Connection: 1 — reader-thought line at the end ("But what if the gift hadn't been given to her?") but no first-person author vulnerability beat, no specific anecdote.
- Reader Engagement: 1 — indirect engagement via the reader-thought line; no direct second-person address.
- Pacing Variety: 1 — short beats present ("The words on the screen were familiar.") but the paragraph still leans medium-dominant.
- Chapter-Ending Momentum: 1 — (implied: ending lands but without a forward-threaded seed).
- **Craft Density: 1** — central image (the phone as a hospital-style cold anchor) is seeded in the opener but does not recur in the middle or closing zones; vulnerability beat from the author is absent from the middle third. One sub-check passes, one fails.
- **Cross-Chapter Craft: 0** — (manuscript-level: Sarah's 2am phone-check is distinct from other chapter images, but the Greek vocabulary still fatigues — same three terms in every chapter).

Total: 6/14. Band: below craft floor — revision required but close to competent.

## Score Level 9

A chapter that scores 9/14 on the rubric looks like this (bestseller-track — the v1.1 target after CRAFT-01..08 enforcement).

**Opening paragraph (paraphrased, scene-first, concrete, central image seeded in zones, vulnerability foreshadowed):**

> The light from Sarah's phone made her kitchen look like a hospital corridor — cold, blue, too bright for 2am. She had read John 10 four times. The fourth read was the worst, because by then the words had stopped meaning anything. She set the phone face-down on the counter, and the sound of it hitting the laminate was louder than it should have been.
>
> *But what if the gift hadn't been given to her?*
>
> I know that 2am. I have a different kitchen, a different verse, and the same silence.

**Why this scores 9:**
- Opening Engagement: 2 — named human, time marker, three sensory details (light colour, sound on laminate, silence).
- Emotional Connection: 2 — italicised reader-thought line immediately followed by a first-person author vulnerability beat ("I know that 2am"). The beat is specific and non-fabricated.
- Reader Engagement: 1 — engagement is mediated through the reader-thought line and the author's "I"; no direct second-person in the opener (the component scores higher later in the chapter).
- Pacing Variety: 2 — short/medium/long distribution across the three paragraph blocks; the single-line italic beat is a deliberate pacing break.
- Chapter-Ending Momentum: 1 — (implied: bestseller-track chapters end with a forward hook; the opener itself doesn't constrain this, so it's scored from later in the chapter).
- **Craft Density: 1** — central image (cold hospital-corridor light) is present in the opening zone AND recurs in the middle and closing zones per the chapter plan; vulnerability beat appears in the opener but the CRAFT-10 middle-third check requires it in the middle third. One sub-check passes (image zonal presence), one partially passes (vulnerability beat is present and seeded but in the opener rather than middle third).
- **Cross-Chapter Craft: 2** — (manuscript-level: images across chapters are distinct — no two chapters share the hospital-corridor-light metaphor; transliterated terms rotate rather than repeat the same three every chapter).

Total: 9/14. Band: competent, edging toward strong. A true 11+ chapter would additionally land the vulnerability beat inside the middle third (Craft Density sub-check 2) rather than front-loading it.

# Chapter 3: Four Ways to Become Strong

You can be strong. This is not wishful thinking — it is a statement about what God has made available to every one of His children. But you must pursue strength deliberately. Strength does not come to the passive believer. It comes to the one who takes specific steps to build it.

Here are four ways to become strong.

**1. Become strong by spending time in the Word of God every day.**

The Bible is not merely a book of information. It is a source of spiritual nourishment. The believer who feeds on the Word daily is the believer who grows strong enough to stand.

> *This book of the law shall not depart out of thy mouth; but thou shalt MEDITATE THEREIN DAY AND NIGHT, that thou mayest observe to do according to all that is written therein: for then thou shalt make thy way prosperous.*
> -- Joshua 1:8

In other words, the strength God promises is connected to the consistent intake of His Word. You cannot be strong on last Sunday's sermon. You need daily bread. Open your Bible. Feed your spirit. Do this without fail.

**2. Become strong by building a consistent life of prayer.**

Prayer is the connection between your weakness and God's power. The believer who prays regularly is drawing on a resource that never runs dry. But prayer requires discipline, and discipline requires a decision.

> *But they that wait upon the Lord shall RENEW THEIR STRENGTH; they shall mount up with wings as eagles.*
> -- Isaiah 40:31

In other words, renewed strength is the direct result of waiting on the Lord. When you pray, you are not simply speaking into the air — you are connecting to the source of all strength. Make prayer your daily discipline, not your emergency option.

**3. Become strong by staying in fellowship with believers who are going forward.**

Years ago, a young man from a farming village enlisted to be a soldier. He arrived at the barracks with enthusiasm but his body was entirely unprepared. When the first long march came, he fell behind. When the first physical test came, his arms were too tired to hold his weapon steady. He had courage enough — what he lacked was the strength that comes only from consistent preparation. He eventually learned this. But he lost months he could not recover. Do not make the same mistake. Strengthen yourself before the battle, not during it!

> *And let us consider one another to provoke unto love and to good works: NOT FORSAKING THE ASSEMBLING OF OURSELVES TOGETHER.*
> -- Hebrews 10:25

In other words, spiritual strength is not built in isolation. You are strengthened by the community of believers around you. Do not stay away from fellowship. It is one of the primary channels through which God strengthens His people.

**4. Become strong by enduring difficulty without quitting.**

Strength is not only built in the quiet seasons. It is built most powerfully in the difficult ones. The believer who endures hardship without abandoning his post comes out of that hardship stronger than before.

> *Wait on the Lord: be of good courage, and he shall STRENGTHEN THINE HEART.*
> -- Psalm 27:14

In other words, endurance is not passivity. It is active waiting — pressing forward in faith when circumstances say stop. Choose to endure. Choose to press. What looks like a test is actually a training session.

Unless you decide to be strong, you cannot have victory.

Strength is a decision before it is a feeling. Decide today. Decide to be in the Word. Decide to pray. Decide to stay in fellowship. Decide to endure. And as you make those decisions, God will meet you with the enabling power that turns a decision into a reality.

May you be strong in the Lord and in the power of His might, from this day forward and in every season to come.

# Chapter 1: The Long Hour

It was just past two when I gave up pretending to sleep. The phone glow over the ceiling kept drawing my eye, a pale rectangle that refused to mean anything kind. I had carried the week in my shoulders and now it was asking to be carried somewhere else. I sat up on the edge of the mattress and let the room hold me for a moment.

I wanted to speak to God the way I had heard others speak — confident, clean, composed. Instead my hands would not stop shaking as I tried to pray, and the first words were less words than breath. I told him I was tired. I told him I did not have a plan. I told him the truth about the long hour and the quiet that had followed it, and slowly, almost without noticing, the room itself felt less hostile. Something in me loosened. The ceiling kept its cold light, and I kept my small honesty, and for once that was enough to begin.

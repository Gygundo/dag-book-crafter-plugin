# Chapter 1: What It Means to Be Strong

Strength, in the Scripture, is not a personality trait and it is not the absence of fear. It is a spiritual capacity - the God-enabled ability to endure pressure, resist the enemy, and advance in your assignment without surrendering. This definition matters. Many believers are confused about what strength is, and that confusion keeps them weak.

## Strength Is Not What the World Calls Strength

The world calls someone strong when they do not need anyone. The world calls someone strong when they can handle everything by themselves. But the Bible calls someone strong when they know where to draw their power from.

> *Finally, my brethren, BE STRONG IN THE LORD, and in the power of his might.*
> -- Ephesians 6:10

In other words, biblical strength is relational. It flows from a connection with the Lord. You are not strong in yourself. You are strong in Him. The believer who cuts himself off from prayer, from the Word, from fellowship - that believer is cutting himself off from the source of his strength.

Think about a vine. A vine that draws from its root will bear fruit in season and out of season. But a vine that has been cut from its root may look healthy for a few days - and then it wilts. This is precisely what happens to the believer who is disconnected from God. Outwardly you can carry on for a season. But your strength is draining, not building. Reconnect to the source today!

## Why You Need a New Definition

You must stop waiting to feel strong before you act. Strength is a decision before it is a feeling. The believer who waits for the feeling will wait a very long time.

The strength God demands is not the product of human effort alone - it flows from a connection with the source of all power.

> *Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go.*
> -- Joshua 1:9 (NASB)

In other words, the command to be strong is always paired with a promise of God's presence. You are not being told to find courage inside yourself. You are being told to draw on the One who is with you. That changes everything. Are you drawing on Him daily? Are you coming to Him in prayer every morning before you face the day? Are you feeding your spirit on the Word before the world makes its demands?

Do not neglect your source. The strength God has made available to you is limitless - but you must access it.

> *The Lord is my strength and my shield; my heart trusted in him, and I am helped.*
> -- Psalm 28:7

Decide today that you will be strong in the Lord. Not in your natural ability. Not in your experience. Not in your reputation. In the Lord. That is where the power is. That is where you must draw from.

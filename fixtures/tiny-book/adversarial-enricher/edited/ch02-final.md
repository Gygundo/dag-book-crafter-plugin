# Chapter 2: Three Reasons Why You Must Be Strong

> *Finally, my brethren, BE STRONG IN THE LORD, and in the power of his might.*
> -- Ephesians 6:10

God commands strength. He does not recommend it. He does not suggest it as an option for the highly motivated. He commands it - and a command from God is a command that carries the enabling power to obey it. Here are three reasons why you cannot afford to remain weak.

**1. You must be strong because the enemy specifically targets the weak.**

> *Be sober, be vigilant; because your adversary the devil, as a ROARING LION, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour.*
> -- 1 Peter 5:8

In other words, the enemy is selective. A lion does not charge into the middle of a healthy herd. It circles the edges. It looks for the straggler - the one lagging behind, the one who has left the group, the one whose attention is elsewhere. The enemy operates the same way. He identifies the believer who is not praying, not in fellowship, not in the Word - and he targets that one.

Are you the straggler? Are you weak because you have drifted from the disciplines that produce strength? Return to them today. Get back to prayer. Get back to the Word. Give the enemy less to work with!

**2. You must be strong because God has commanded it and His commands carry enabling power.**

When you read "be strong in the Lord", you are not reading an ideal to aspire to. You are reading a command that God has already provided the supply for. He does not command what He has not also resourced.

> *He giveth power to the faint; and to them that have no might he INCREASETH STRENGTH.*
> -- Isaiah 40:29

In other words, the supply is already there. Your weakness is not disqualifying - it is an invitation. God meets the weak person who comes to Him and increases their strength. But you must come to Him. You must draw on what He has made available. God does not command what He does not also enable.

**3. You must be strong because others around you are drawing from your example.**

You are not alone in your assignment. The young believers in your church are watching how you carry pressure. Your family is drawing silent conclusions from whether you stand or crumble. When you are strong, you give others permission and courage to be strong.

> *Strengthen ye the weak hands, and confirm the FEEBLE KNEES. Say to them that are of a fearful heart, Be strong, fear not.*
> -- Isaiah 35:3-4

In other words, your strength has a ministry to others. Strengthening yourself is never only a personal matter. It is a service to every person connected to you.

The enemy is hunting weak believers. God has provided enabling strength. Others need to see you stand. There is no legitimate reason to remain weak.

May you receive today the strength of the Lord that overwhelms every opposition and carries you through to total victory.

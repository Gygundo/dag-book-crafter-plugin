# Chapter 2: The Kitchen Light

Morning came before I was ready for it. I pushed the kettle onto the stove and watched a yellow pool on the kitchen tile widen as the overhead bulb warmed up. Everything in the house still smelled faintly of the night before, of damp coats and unfinished conversations and the tea I had never drunk. I stood there with my palms flat on the counter and let the floor take some of my weight.

There is a kind of prayer that happens when no one is watching, and that morning I had no speech left for it — only the steady noise of water beginning to move and a slow willingness to be found. I thought about the people I had been short with and the ones I had not answered. I thought about mercy as a place you walk into rather than a feeling you earn. The pool on the tile grew wider, and the day, to my surprise, did not refuse me.

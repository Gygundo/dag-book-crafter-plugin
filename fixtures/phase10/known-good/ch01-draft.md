# Chapter 1: The Beginning

Sarah sat by the window at 6:47am, watching steam curl from her chipped blue mug. The kitchen tile was cold under her bare feet. She had not slept.

The Greek word for grace is *charis*, and it carries a weight the English word never quite manages. It was not earned. It was given. Yet the giving did not make it cheap.

> *"But what if grace really is enough for me right now?"*

Three weeks earlier, she had stood in this same kitchen and said the words aloud for the first time. I am not okay. The cup had trembled in her hands. So had her voice.

There is a power in honesty that the polished version of ourselves can never touch. Paul called this kind of power *dunamis* — a strength that arrives precisely when our own runs out.

> *"If God already knows, why is it so hard to say it out loud?"*

The morning light shifted across the floor. Outside, a neighbour's dog barked twice and went quiet. Sarah lifted the mug, took a sip, and let the warmth go through her. Tomorrow would still be tomorrow. But today she was here.

That is where every real change begins.

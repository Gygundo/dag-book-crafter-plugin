# Chapter 1: The Pulpit Seam

Sarah sat by the window at 6:47am, watching steam curl from her chipped blue mug. She had not slept. The kitchen tile was cold under her bare feet.

The Greek word for grace is *charis*. It carries a weight the English word does not. Quiet, unearned, unrelenting.

> *"But what if grace really is enough for me right now?"*

So let us consider what this means for the woman in the kitchen. The honest version of ourselves is the only version God has ever wanted to meet. Paul said as much, in different words, more than once.

> *"Why is it so hard to say it out loud?"*

The morning light shifted across the floor. The dog next door barked twice. Sarah took a sip of coffee and stayed where she was.

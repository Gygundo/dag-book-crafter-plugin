# Chapter 2: Too Many Words

Sarah set the mug down at 6:48am and watched the steam stop. The light through the curtains had a yellow edge to it she had not noticed before. The day was beginning whether she was ready or not.

The Greek word for grace, *charis*, is only one of the words she had been taught.

There was *agape* — the love that does not bargain. The love that does not flinch when it sees you exactly as you are.

There was *dunamis* — the power that arrives in the moment of weakness, not before.

There was *exousia* — the authority a daughter walks in, not the begging of a slave.

> *"What if all four of these have been true the whole time?"*

She read the verse again. Slowly this time.

> *"What changes if I actually believe it today?"*

The kettle clicked off behind her. Outside, a child laughed in the next garden. Sarah closed the book gently and stood.
